## 🔍 QUICK REFERENCE: KEY QUESTIONS ANSWERED

### Legal/Regulatory

**Q: Do I need a license to trade my own money?**
A: Not in most jurisdictions. Individual proprietary traders don't need licenses. However, if you use leverage, check with your local regulator and broker.

**Q: What about taxes?**
A: You must report all trading profits as income. Consult a tax professional for your specific jurisdiction. In the US, use Form 8949 and Schedule D.

**Q: Can my broker refuse my algorithmic trading?**
A: Potentially, but most brokers allow individual algorithmic trading. Check their Terms of Service. Document confirmation.

**Q: What happens if I lose money?**
A: Trading losses are your responsibility. They're tax-deductible in some jurisdictions but don't justify poor risk management.

### System & Trading

**Q: What's the minimum I should start with?**
A: Start with an amount you can afford to lose completely. Minimum varies by broker (often $100-500), but consider starting with $1,000-$5,000 to learn.

**Q: How much should I risk per trade?**
A: Standard: 1-2% of account per trade. Conservative: 0.5-1%. Never more than 2%.

**Q: What if my system fails?**
A: Design for graceful degradation. Manual override should always be possible. This is why extensive testing matters.

**Q: How often should I monitor?**
A: Minimum daily during trading hours. Better: hourly checks. Weekly comprehensive review required.

### Personal

**Q: What if I can't handle the emotions?**
A: Start small. Build discipline over time. Consider paper trading first. Remember: losses are normal and expected.

**Q: What if I override my system?**
A: Document why. Most often, overriding loses money. The personal agreement helps you stay committed.

**Q: How do I know my system works?**
A: Extensive backtesting (5+ years), out-of-sample testing, walk-forward analysis, paper trading. Never assume—test everything.

**Q: Can I modify my system mid-trading?**
A: Only through formal testing process. Ad-hoc changes usually make things worse. Follow the modification checklist.
